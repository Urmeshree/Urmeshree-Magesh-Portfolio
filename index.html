About me

A biotechnology foundation, shaped into a bioinformatics career focused on neurodegeneration and data-driven biology.

I started my journey in Biotechnology, learning how experiments are designed, how samples behave in the lab, and why details like controls, timing, and sample handling matter. Through internships in molecular biology, microbiology, and structural bioinformatics, I worked with techniques such as PCR, SDS-PAGE, microbiological screening, collagen extraction, and virtual screening of small molecules.

During my Master’s in Bioinformatics at Northeastern University, I shifted my focus to computational analysis, working with bulk RNA-seq, single-cell / single-nucleus data, and spatial transcriptomics. In the Butovsky Lab, I analyze Alzheimer’s disease datasets to characterize cell-type specific changes, ligand–receptor communication, and pathway activity in microglia, astrocytes, and other brain cells.

I also enjoy working with clinical and operational data—for example, analyzing AACT clinical trial datasets and hospital records using SQL, R, Python, Power BI, and R Shiny to build dashboards that make complex data intuitive.

Long term, I’m excited about roles where I can combine experimental awareness with computational rigor to support translational research, biomarker discovery, and better decision-making in neurology and oncology.
